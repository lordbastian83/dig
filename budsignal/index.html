How signals work

The full rule set. There is no discretionary step — what you read here is exactly what the code does.

1Trend cross

A candidate forms when the 20-period EMA crosses the 50-period EMA on the 4-hour chart. Cross up → long candidate; cross down → short candidate. The signal candle itself must close in the cross's direction.

2Regime gates

Longs are only taken above the 200-period EMA and shorts only below it; ADX 14 must read 20 or higher (EMA crosses whipsaw in chop); and the 50-EMA itself must already be sloping the trade's way over the last 12 hours.

3Momentum & volume

RSI 14 must confirm without being exhausted: 45–70 for longs, 30–55 for shorts. Volume above its 20-period average raises confidence. Stops sit 1.5 × ATR 14 from entry, targets 2 × ATR — and once price moves 1 × ATR in favor, the stop moves to breakeven.

◆Breakout stream

A second, independent strategy: long when price closes above its 55-candle high, short below its 55-candle low, exiting on a 2×ATR trailing stop (no fixed target, max 3 days). The only rule set here that stayed profitable out-of-sample across 3 years — see the research report.

44-hour entry window

A signal is valid for one candle — four hours from the close that produced it. If unfilled, it expires. Outcomes are scored over the following 24 hours: target, stop, or expiry at market. Signals are evaluated on closed candles only, so they never repaint mid-candle.
