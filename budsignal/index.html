How signals work

The full rule set. There is no discretionary step — what you read here is exactly what the code does.

1Trend cross

A candidate forms when the 20-period EMA crosses the 50-period EMA on the 4-hour chart. Cross up → long candidate; cross down → short candidate.

2Momentum filter

RSI 14 must confirm without being exhausted: between 45 and 70 for longs, between 30 and 55 for shorts. Crosses outside these bands are discarded.

3Volume & sizing

Volume above its 20-period average raises confidence. Stops sit 1.5 × ATR 14 from entry and targets 2 × ATR, so risk scales with current volatility.

44-hour entry window

A signal is valid for one candle — four hours from the close that produced it. If unfilled, it expires. Outcomes are scored over the following 24 hours: target, stop, or expiry at market.
